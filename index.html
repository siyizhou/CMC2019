An Investigation of Labeled Data Reduction on Voice Conversion with Conditional Generative Adversarial Networks

Minchuan Chen, Weijian Hou, Jun Ma, Shaojun Wang, Jing Xiao

Ping An Technology, China

Abstract

Recent studies have shown remarkable success in voice conversion (VC) based on generative adversarial networks (GANs) without parallel data. In this paper, based on the conditional generative adversarial network (CGAN), we propose a self-supervised and semi-supervised method combined with mixup technique that allows non-parallel many-to-many voice conversion with fewer labeled data. In our method, the discriminator of CGAN not only learns to distinguish real/fake samples, but also learns to classify them. The discriminator can be used to predict labels for the unlabeled samples and thus reducing the reliance on labeled data. Following this approach, we only need to provide a small amount of labeled data for voice conversion, which enables single generative network to implement many-to-many mapping between different voice domains and generate converted speech as quickly as real time. We verify that the proposed approach is able to achieve comparable voice quality and speaker similarity with only 5% of the labeled data.

Speech Samples

The voice conversion experiments are conducted on the VCTK dataset and each speaker recorded 400 sentences.
Baseline-100: Voice converted by the vanilla StarGAN-VC model with 100% labeled data.
Baseline-50: Voice converted by the vanilla StarGAN-VC model with 50% labeled data.
Baseline-10: Voice converted by the vanilla StarGAN-VC model with 10% labeled data.
Proposed-10: Voice converted by the proposed method with only 10% labeled data.
Proposed-5: Voice converted by the proposed method with only 5% labeled data.
